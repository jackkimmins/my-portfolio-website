About Me

Hello, my name is Jack Kimmins. I am passionate about building things and, more importantly, finding innovative ways to solve existing problems. Throughout the years, the world of computing has always inspired me due to the vast variety and the endless possibilities from every unique area of the subject.

However, my primary interest in computing would certainly be programming/scripting. I gain great satisfaction from creating desktop and web applications, linking them with a database and designing beautiful UIs to complement their backends. A few of my favourite ventures are listed below in the projects segment.

After graduating with a degree in Computing, I aim to acquire a master's degree in Computer Science and to continue my own endeavours to learn more about this fantastic subject, both in my personal time and in different work placements throughout the coming years.

360

360 is a panorama photograph viewer that I designed and developed to showcase my personal 360 images. It can read the EXIF metadata of uploaded images to automatically determine the picture's location, when it was taken etc. and display this information on the user's screen.
PHP 8 MySQL HTML5 EXIF REST APIs

Offendermetre

The Offendermetre is my implementation of a profanity filter. It automatically censors offensive words or phrases in a given text. Due to the Scunthorpe Problem, standard word filters are not completely fool proof. However, my implementation uses machine learning and can tackle some of the hardest phrases to solve, including misspellings and borderline cases.
C# ML.NET Sentiment Classification

jTest

jTest is an integrated reinforcement learning platform designed to promote learning in a fun and competitive manner. Reinforcing an understanding of a particular subject enables students to become more successful in real-world situations. This is done by testing students on teacher defined questions and rewarding them with jPoints for correct answers and deducting them for incorrect answers.
PHP Bootstrap MySQL
